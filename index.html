What is therapy

Counselling provides an opportunity to explore personal difﬁculties in a conﬁdential and supportive environment. This may include expressing feelings that are painful, which many of us experience at certain times in our live. When this happens, it can be difﬁcult to stay positive and cope with everyday life. Counselling provides a safe and non-judgemental space to explore these feeling, identifying why you feel the way you do, and encouraging you to find your own solutions and move towards self-awareness and personal growth.

Who am I?

Personal and professional experience

Hello, my name is Rosie.
I am a qualified counsellor with a degree in Counselling and a CPCAB Level 5 Accredited Certificate in Counselling Children and Young People. Through the use of age-appropriate counselling, I aim to empower my clients to express their feelings and help them bring clarity to their experiences.

I am an integrative counsellor who leans on a person-centred approach, working collaboratively with my clients to use interventions based on what works best for them and the issues they bring to therapy. I specialise in working with adolescents and young people, and I enjoy using creativity in my work where clients find this helpful.

What is online/telephone counselling?

Online/telephone counselling gives you the opportunity to access counselling at a time and in a place that is convenient to you. It can help you with a wide range of issues such as anxiety, stress, depression, loss, relationship issues, loneliness, identity, self-esteem and confidence.

It may not be suitable for everyone but it is an alternative to face-to-face interaction and can be just as helpful. I will advise you if face-to-face counselling, or some other form of support, might be more suitable for you.

Can I attend a session with my child?

Yes, I aim to meet with parents/guardians and their child in an initial meeting before counselling begins. In some cases, it is appropriate (or necessary) for parents/guardians to attend on occasions when sessions are reviewed. This gives parents/guardians to opportunity to ask questions and discuss any concerns they have.

What should I expect in the first session?

During our first session we will discuss your reasons for coming to counselling and possible goals you would like to work towards. This session will be more about getting to know each other so that we may decide if we would like to work with each other.
